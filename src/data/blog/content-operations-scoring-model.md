## How the Content Operations scoring model is structured

The Content Operations scoring model is designed to help marketing leads in Austin's small service businesses understand and improve their content operations. It's structured around key categories that drive content success: Strategy, Planning, Creation, Distribution, and Measurement.

Each category is scored on a scale of 0-100, with higher scores indicating better performance. Let's dive into how these scores are calculated and what they mean for your business.

## What each score band actually means

Scores are banded into the following ranges to provide clear guidance on where your business stands:

- **0-30: Critical** - Your content operations are significantly underperforming and require immediate attention.

- **31-60: Poor** - There are major gaps in your content operations that need to be addressed to see improvement.

- **61-80: Fair** - Your content operations are functional but have room for significant improvement.

- **81-90: Good** - Your content operations are performing well but could still benefit from some fine-tuning.

- **91-100: Excellent** - Your content operations are best-in-class and serve as a model for others to follow.

## Examples of scoring tradeoffs in practice

Scoring isn't always straightforward, as there can be tradeoffs between different aspects of content operations. For example, investing heavily in content creation might boost your 'Creation' score but lower your 'Planning' score if it leads to rushed, poorly-planned content.

Consider a local Austin HVAC business that scores high in 'Creation' (95) but low in 'Planning' (25). While their content is high-quality, the lack of planning leads to inconsistent publishing schedules and missed opportunities. To improve their overall score, they might need to invest more in planning, even if it temporarily lowers their 'Creation' score.

## How to use scores to set priorities

To set priorities for improvement, focus on your lowest-scoring categories. Addressing these areas first will have the biggest impact on your overall content operations score.

For example, if your 'Measurement' score is significantly lower than the others, focus on implementing better tracking and analytics. This will help you make data-driven decisions and continuously improve your content operations.
