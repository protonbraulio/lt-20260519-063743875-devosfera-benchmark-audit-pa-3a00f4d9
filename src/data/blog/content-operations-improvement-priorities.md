## Findings that should move to the top of the list

Austin's marketing leads should prioritize addressing these key findings to significantly improve content operations:

1. **Inconsistent Content Quality**: Ensure all content meets the same high standard by implementing a consistent review process.

2. **Lack of Clear Ownership**: Establish a clear owner for each content piece to ensure accountability and timely completion.

3. **Inefficient Content Approval**: Streamline the approval process by reducing approval steps and clarifying roles.

## Weaknesses that create the biggest score drag

These weaknesses have the most significant impact on Austin's content operations score and should be tackled next:

1. **Slow Content Creation**: Improve turnaround times by optimizing content creation processes and providing adequate resources.

2. **Poor Content Localization**: Enhance local relevance by investing in local content creation and tailoring existing content to Austin's audience.

3. **Inadequate Content Performance Tracking**: Implement a robust analytics system to track content performance and make data-driven decisions.

## A remediation order that reduces wasted work

To minimize wasted effort, Austin's marketing leads should follow this remediation order:

1. **Establish Clear Processes**: Document and communicate clear processes for content creation, approval, and distribution.

2. **Train Teams**: Provide training to ensure all team members understand their roles and responsibilities in the content operations process.

3. **Implement Technology**: Invest in tools that automate repetitive tasks and facilitate collaboration.

## What to remeasure after improvements

After implementing these improvements, Austin's marketing leads should remeasure the following metrics to ensure progress:

1. **Content Turnaround Time**: Track the time it takes from content initiation to publication.

2. **Content Quality Score**: Assess the consistency and quality of published content using a standardized scoring system.

3. **Local Engagement**: Measure the level of engagement with locally tailored content.

### How do you know when content operations needs improvement?

Look for repeated clarification requests, unclear handoffs, inconsistent completion times, missing data, avoidable rework, or teams using different definitions.
